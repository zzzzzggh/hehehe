# PAC-51-R29F-META-R5H-GLOBAL-PORTFOLIO-ARITHMETIC-DECISION-R1F

Date: 2026-07-20

## 1. Stage boundary

This stage performs one finite cross-mechanism Portfolio arithmetic decision using at most eighteen E4-quality or E4-proxy signals across at most six mechanism families.

It authorizes no named product, game concept, feature set, price, page, outreach, payment, KYC, prototype or build.

The result may grant at most two **mechanism-level structural licenses**. A structural license only authorizes shallow representative generation and symmetric challenge. It is not a product-depth, validation, deployment or build license.

## 2. Compared mechanism families

1. G — Buyout single-player game catalog
2. A — Consumer paid or subscription app portfolio
3. B — Self-published book and knowledge catalog
4. C — Creative-asset and stock-media catalog
5. X — Platform-native B2B extensions and add-ons
6. P — Narrow usage-metered API or data-monitoring utility

White-label/channel licensing was not included in the arithmetic set because R1E found no sufficiently comparable ordinary-vendor distribution. It remains an orthogonal challenger, not a hidden seventh finalist.

## 3. Eighteen-signal evidence cap

### G — Buyout games

- G1: Historical VGI distribution: median lifetime indie-game gross revenue about USD 3,947; more than two-thirds below USD 10,000; about one fifth above USD 50,000. This is older and platform-estimated, therefore Q2 and discounted.
- G2: 2023 Steam releases showed median first-year revenue about 2.64 times first-week revenue for games clearing minimal launch filters. Long tails exist, but most lifetime value still depends on launch traction.
- G3: A 23,485-game study found 48% delayed release, with a 14-day median delay. Production schedules are uncertain before commercial uncertainty is considered.

### A — Consumer apps

- A1: RevenueCat 2025: newly launched top-5% apps earned USD 8,880 after year one while the bottom quartile earned no more than USD 19.
- A2: RevenueCat 2026: only 17.3% of newly launched subscription apps reached USD 1,000 monthly revenue within two years and 4.6% reached USD 10,000; pre-2020 apps still generated 69% of subscription revenue.
- A3: Apple App Store empirical research found popularity spillovers inside developer portfolios but a negative quality effect from portfolio diversity.

### B — Books and knowledge catalogs

- B1: 2025 indie-author survey: roughly 80% of authors with one to four books earned under USD 100 per month; authors with 25+ books reported about USD 3,000 median monthly income and over 40% exceeded USD 5,000.
- B2: Marketing spend rose with catalog size: reported average monthly spend was USD 75 for one to two books and USD 1,594 for 25-49 books.
- B3: 2026 author survey: 30% reported declining businesses; growing authors were more likely to accelerate output and maintain owned newsletters. Catalog income remains an active publishing-and-distribution business.

### C — Creative assets and stock media

- C1: Adobe Stock pays 33% for photos/vectors/illustrations and 35% for video; large-plan minimum royalties can be USD 0.33-0.38 per standard license.
- C2: Envato reports more than 81,000 creators have earned and more than USD 1.3 billion has been paid cumulatively, but does not disclose an ordinary-author income distribution; new author applications were closed at the time of review.
- C3: Envato subscriber-share allocates revenue according to actual subscriber usage; Fab pays an 88% sales share. Unit economics can be good, but discovery, continued relevance and portfolio usage remain unmeasured.

### X — Platform-native B2B extensions

- X1: MicroConf 2024 independent-SaaS survey: 28% of companies were below USD 1,000 MRR and 65% had only one to ten paying customers. A small customer count can generate meaningful recurring revenue, but the sample is survivor-selected.
- X2: Shopify reported more than 16,000 apps and over USD 1 billion paid to developers in the prior year. Revenue-share rules changed from an annual to a lifetime USD 1 million exemption, proving both ecosystem value and unilateral policy risk.
- X3: Atlassian aggregates its Forge incentive across all apps owned by a partner; app partners remain responsible for support and maintenance, while Connect-to-Forge migration and revenue-share changes create correlated portfolio maintenance.

### P — Usage-metered APIs and data utilities

- P1: A documented small-provider case grew from about USD 10 per month during its first six months to about USD 1,500 per month, reporting roughly 90% margin after marketplace commission. This is Q3 single-case evidence.
- P2: RapidAPI increased its marketplace fee to 25%, pays providers after final settlement on a delayed schedule and offsets refunds or disputes. Marketplace distribution is not free and creates platform dependence.
- P3: A 2.2-million client-API dependency study supports Hyrum-law behavior: with enough clients, observable behavior becomes depended upon. Narrow interfaces reduce but do not remove compatibility obligations.

## 4. Explicit arithmetic model

The model is a decision aid, not an observed return forecast.

### 4.1 Common target

The reference target is **USD 500 per month mature owner net cash flow** from a mechanism family, after platform fees and routine acquisition cash, but before personal tax. Founder development time is not treated as cash cost; it is measured separately as irreversible attention.

### 4.2 Production-time assumptions

Because comparable cohort data do not report production hours, the following are explicit model assumptions and receive uncertainty penalties:

| Family | Assumed founder-weeks per ordinary asset | Mature maintenance prior |
|---|---:|---|
| Buyout game | 12-40 | low to moderate per title; compatibility and community tail |
| Consumer app | 2-8 | moderate; OS, store, churn and support |
| Book | 8-20 | low technical maintenance; continuing marketing/output |
| Creative-asset pack | 0.5-4 | low to moderate; relevance and compatibility |
| B2B extension | 6-20 | moderate to high unless scope and support are tightly bounded |
| API/data utility | 4-12 | moderate; uptime, source stability and client compatibility |

### 4.3 Hit-count arithmetic

- Consumer apps: with a 17.3% two-year probability of reaching USD 1,000 monthly revenue, an independence model requires **13 launches** for a 90% chance of at least one hit. Shared store, category and acquisition risks make the real requirement likely higher.
- Buyout games: using the older estimate that roughly 20% exceed USD 50,000 lifetime gross, an independence model requires **11 releases** for a 90% chance of at least one such outcome. At 12-40 founder-weeks each, this implies roughly 132-440 founder-weeks before correlated genre and distribution risk.
- Books: the clearest observable transition appears around **25+ books**, not one to three. The reported USD 3,000 median monthly income for 25+ books is associated with materially higher marketing spend and self-selection.
- Creative assets: at Adobe's USD 0.33 minimum royalty, USD 500 monthly requires about **1,516 monthly licenses** before other costs. No reliable median downloads per ordinary portfolio were found, so required asset count is not estimable.
- B2B extensions: one asset can exceed the USD 500 target with a small paying-customer count, but ordinary-entry hit probability and support hours are not reported. The arithmetic advantage is revenue density, not proven ease of entry.
- APIs/data utilities: one narrow utility can exceed the target, as the Q3 case demonstrates, but there is no cohort-level hit rate. The arithmetic advantage is high gross margin and usage billing; the penalty is severe evidence uncertainty.

## 6. Mechanism decisions

### 6.1 SL-A — GRANTED

**Mechanism:** Platform-native B2B extensions and add-ons.

**Why it survives:**

- integrated buyer discovery, installation and recurring billing;
- one asset can create meaningful cash flow with few customers;
- strong fit with the user's AI-software development capability;
- possible portfolio reuse across a coherent platform/workflow family;
- potential transferability as a small software asset.

**Binding conditions:**

- public or marketplace distribution; no enterprise outbound-sales dependency;
- platform-managed installation and billing where possible;
- no open-ended implementation, migration or custom consulting;
- no regulated or high-liability decision output;
- narrow permission and data surface;
- first-line support must be bounded by product design and documentation;
- no family may rely on a single platform without an explicit concentration cap;
- existing pages, emails or prior product ideas confer no advantage.

This is a **mechanism structural license only**.

### 6.2 SL-B — GRANTED, CONDITIONAL AND CAPPED

**Mechanism:** Narrow usage-metered API or data-monitoring utilities with an existing discovery/billing rail.

**Why it survives:**

- high gross-margin potential;
- usage-based payment matches machine-consumed value;
- strong fit with the user's technical capability;
- self-serve delivery and low marginal fulfilment;
- can form a coherent family around shared ingestion, normalization, metering and monitoring infrastructure.

**Binding conditions:**

- a real search, API marketplace, integration or embedded-distribution path must exist before representative advancement;
- no generic API with no demand-capture mechanism;
- narrow and versioned public surface;
- no mission-critical SLA, regulated personal data or irreversible customer dependency;
- source rights and upstream stability must be explicit;
- strict usage limits and cost pass-through controls;
- maximum cumulative mechanism attention remains below 20%;
- the weak cohort evidence is carried as an uncertainty penalty, not ignored.

This is a **conditional mechanism structural license**, not a product or build license.

### 6.3 No structural license

- Consumer apps: distribution hit rate requires a multi-app factory, but maintenance and store correlation make a thirteen-launch arithmetic unattractive before a specific natural-discovery mechanism is shown.
- Creative assets: production and fulfilment are attractive, but ordinary earnings and required portfolio size remain unmeasurable; low royalties imply large usage volume.
- Buyout games: full search rights are preserved, but eleven-release hit arithmetic and long production cycles fail the current attention-reversibility comparison. This is not a permanent rejection of games.
- Books: the strongest catalog evidence requires roughly 25+ titles and continuing marketing; it does not fit the user's current comparative advantage or rapid-recovery objective.

These families remain symmetric challengers. They cannot be reactivated merely because a striking success case appears.
