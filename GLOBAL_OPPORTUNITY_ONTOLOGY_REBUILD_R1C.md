# PAC-51-R29F-META-R5H-GLOBAL-OPPORTUNITY-ONTOLOGY-REBUILD-R1C

Date: 2026-07-20

## 1. Stage boundary

This stage rebuilds the global opportunity space. It compares domains, asset forms, monetization structures, distribution structures and lifecycle obligations. It does **not** authorize any named product, game concept, feature set, price, page, outreach, payment, KYC, prototype or build.

The prior `GLOBAL-MECHANISM-FRONTIER-R1` is downgraded to `PROVISIONAL / B2B-DIGITAL-TOOL BIASED`. Its evidence remains usable, but its ranking cannot allocate candidate-specific depth.

## 2. Six-axis opportunity ontology

Every future opportunity must be represented by a complete fingerprint across six axes.

### Axis A — Payer
Enterprise; SMB; professional; developer; creator; consumer; family; school/institution; channel/reseller; platform; advertiser.

### Axis B — Purchase motive
Save time; reduce cost; increase revenue; reduce risk; comply; convenience; entertainment; emotional experience; social/identity; learning; creation; collection/aesthetics; competition/achievement; information/discovery.

### Axis C — Asset form
Software; app; API; plugin/extension; data/intelligence; monitoring; game/interactive experience; content/media; course; creative asset/template; IP/catalog; community; marketplace; software-plus-light-operations.

### Axis D — Monetization
Buyout; subscription; usage; advertising; IAP; DLC; transaction fee; lead/affiliate fee; license; royalty; publisher/platform revenue share; engagement share; membership; sponsorship; hybrid.

### Axis E — Distribution ownership
Owned search; app/extension marketplace; game store; content/asset marketplace; algorithmic feed; channel/reseller; integration ecosystem; social/viral; community; publisher; direct sales; paid acquisition; cross-sell.

### Axis F — Lifecycle
Static long-tail; low-frequency compatibility; periodic data refresh; periodic content refresh; customer configuration; rule/regulation updates; live service; multiplayer server; community moderation; high-frequency trend chasing.

## 6. Game-specific decomposition

Games are not a single mechanism. The following remain separate search lanes:

- buyout long-tail single-player catalog;
- system-driven replayable single-player games;
- narrative/puzzle/experience buyout games;
- domestic mini-game IAA;
- domestic mini-game IAP/hybrid;
- UGC platform engagement share;
- paid mobile single-player games;
- HTML5 distribution/licensing;
- publisher/subscription-library licensing;
- DLC, soundtrack and derivative assets;
- asynchronous/light-online games;
- real-time multiplayer/live-service games;
- MMO/gacha/large live service, currently hard-exit.

Game evaluation must add production cycle, pre-release testability, content intensity, system reuse, art/audio cost, porting, server dependence, moderation, paid acquisition dependence, revenue half-life, hit rate and portfolio diversification.

**Structural Coverage Gate: PASS.**

### Economic evidence coverage

Official platform rails confirm that these opportunity spaces exist, but ordinary-later-entrant economics, failure rates, maintenance tails and portfolio hit-rate economics are not yet balanced across all superfamilies.

**Economic Evidence Gate: NOT YET PASSED.**

Therefore no unified ranking and no candidate-specific depth are authorized.

## 8. Evidence maturity levels

- E0: conceptual mechanism only
- E1: official platform/distribution/payment rail confirmed
- E2: ordinary later-entrant revenue and effort evidence
- E3: failure, exit and maintenance-tail evidence
- E4: multi-asset portfolio economics and correlation evidence

R1C establishes E1 coverage across the major superfamilies. R1D must purchase E2-E4 evidence before any formal frontier ranking.
