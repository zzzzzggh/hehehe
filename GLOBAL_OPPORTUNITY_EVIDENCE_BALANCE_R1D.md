## 6. Cross-wave findings

### Platform rails are abundant; ordinary economics are scarce

App stores, enterprise marketplaces, game stores, creator platforms, course platforms, stock-media markets and billing systems all provide real rails. A rail does not establish median owner cash flow, acquisition efficiency or founder detachment.

### Income distributions are repeatedly right-skewed

Subscription apps, games, algorithmic creator platforms and affiliate assets all show winner concentration or low ordinary-entry success. Aggregate market growth and headline cases cannot raise a mechanism's score without E2 and E3 evidence.

### Static catalogs remain structurally attractive but lack E4 proof

Books, stock media, creative assets, digital downloads and buyout games support automatic sale and delivery. The unresolved question is portfolio arithmetic: asset count, production cost, median revenue, decay and maintenance correlation without a breakout hit.

### Platform-native B2B software has real demand capture but service tails

WPS, Shopify and Atlassian provide discovery, installation and billing. The same evidence reveals certification, migration, customer-service, policy and compatibility burdens.

### UGC, mini-game and media mechanisms are operational businesses

Their income depends on engagement, retention, content cadence, policy compliance or community activity. They must not be evaluated as passive catalog assets.

### Transaction and compliance lanes fail the current early-asset risk fit

Marketplaces add multi-sided acquisition and financial liability. Compliance software adds changing rules, documentation and interpretation risk. Both retain search rights but receive no candidate depth.

## 9. Search-attention update

The next stage must target missing E2 and E4, not collect more platform documentation.

Priority evidence lanes:
1. Buyout games and static catalogs — median revenue, production cycle, portfolio size and decay.
2. Consumer paid apps — ordinary distribution, acquisition cost, maintenance and multi-app catalogs.
3. Creative assets, stock media, books and courses — ordinary contributor earnings and catalog correlation.
4. Enterprise extensions — small-vendor revenue, support hours, churn and migration costs.
5. Data/monitoring and usage APIs — distribution ownership, margins and customer concentration.
6. White-label/channel licensing — repeatable channel access and transfer of first-line support.

Capped lanes:
- mini-game, UGC and engagement-share experiences;
- media, algorithmic content and affiliate assets;
- compliance software;
- transaction marketplaces;
- productized service progressing toward software.
