# PAC-51-R29F-META-R5H-GLOBAL-MECHANISM-FRONTIER-R1

Date: 2026-07-20

## 1. Stage boundary

This stage compares **cash-flow mechanism families**, not named products.

It authorizes no candidate-specific page, price, repository, outreach, payment, KYC, customer-data request, prototype or build.

The output is an initial search-attention frontier, not a declaration that any family or project is globally top-tier.

## 2. Objective and dimensions

Objective: maximize long-term, stable, low-maintenance, automatable or delegable owner net cash flow across a portfolio while bounding founder attention, irreversible capital and correlated platform risk.

Each family receives a structural-prior score on eleven dimensions. Scores are model judgments from 1 to 5 and are not measurements of realized returns.

| Dimension | Weight |
|---|---:|
| Natural demand capture | 12 |
| Independent payment event | 8 |
| Ordinary later-entrant economics | 12 |
| Owner cash-flow density and ceiling | 14 |
| Automation ceiling | 10 |

| Founder detachment | 10 |
| Replication efficiency | 7 |
| Portfolio maintenance correlation | 5 |
| China execution fit | 6 |
| Capital and attention reversibility | 6 |
| **Total** | **100** |

## 4. Evidence-supported mechanism observations

### F1 — Platform-native software extensions and add-ons

The mechanism has real platform discovery and integrated payment rails. Shopify allows public apps to be installed by multiple merchants and supports one-time or recurring charges; developers keep 100% of the first USD 1 million of gross app revenue under the current programme, subject to processing fees. Atlassian pays marketplace partners a percentage of gross app revenue and applies different revenue-share rates by framework. Google Workspace and WPS both operate public application marketplaces with review and installation flows.

This confirms a genuine distribution-and-payment mechanism, but not easy success. Public review, security, privacy, platform APIs, support requirements and ecosystem concentration create correlated maintenance risk.

### F2 — Marketplace-native static digital assets

Etsy permits seller-designed digital downloads and handles digital VAT collection in many buyer jurisdictions. The mechanism has low delivery cost and integrated checkout, but sellers face listing, transaction and payment-processing fees. The R1 inference is that the mechanism is excellent for replication and reversibility but must be capped because ordinary cash-flow density can be low and marketplace similarity can compress pricing.

### F3 — Standalone usage-metered utility or API

Stripe provides native usage ingestion, metering, recurring prices, invoices and usage alerts. Therefore billing infrastructure is no longer the key constraint. The dominant unknown is distribution ownership: a technically excellent utility without a search, marketplace or integration channel can remain invisible. F3 may only receive future depth when a separate demand-capture path is demonstrated at mechanism level.

### F4 — Narrow vertical workflow subscription software

Recurring subscriptions and workflow embedding can create higher cash-flow density than static assets. The structural penalty is the support tail: configuration, onboarding, migrations, exceptions and churn can turn a small software asset into a service business. R1 keeps this family on the frontier but below self-serve and channel-distributed mechanisms.

### F5 — Recurring data, monitoring or intelligence subscription

Official public APIs such as the SEC EDGAR XBRL APIs make some raw data freely accessible. This weakens businesses whose only value is access. The mechanism remains attractive when value comes from normalization, entity resolution, change detection, prioritization, workflow insertion or alerts. Data rights, source stability and false-positive support remain mandatory gates.

### F6 — Compliance evidence or continuous-monitoring software

Regulations can create recurring tasks. DORA requires covered financial entities to establish and review testing programmes and conduct appropriate tests at least yearly. The EU AI Act introduces documentation, logging, monitoring and transparency obligations on a phased timeline. These sources support recurring demand, but also show why the mechanism has a high legal and maintenance tail: obligations, guidance and timelines can change, and software must avoid presenting itself as legal assurance.

### F7 — White-label or licensed modules through channels

This mechanism separates production from customer acquisition: a reusable module can be sold or licensed through agencies, integrators, resellers or ecosystem partners. It has strong owner-detachment potential when installation, first-line support and client-specific configuration remain with the channel. The unresolved issue is not technical feasibility but whether ordinary small entrants can secure repeatable channel access without enterprise selling.

### F8 — Productized service progressing toward software

The mechanism can reveal real demand earlier than pure software, but it is vulnerable to false progress: revenue can be created by founder labour, exceptions and custom delivery. No depth is allowed unless the service has a pre-declared automation and delegation path, a hard support cap and evidence that unit economics remain positive after replacing founder labour.

### F9 — Embedded payments or transaction take-rate

Stripe Connect supports application fees, payment markups, routing and revenue share. It also shows the scale requirement: Stripe's partner revenue-share route requires qualifying integrations and a payment-volume run rate, while platforms that set their own pricing bear per-account, payout, dispute and risk-management responsibilities. This is a strong mature-company layer, not a preferred first low-capital portfolio asset.

### F10 — Contingent recovery or audit revenue share

The mechanism aligns price with recovered value, but the earlier search repeatedly found sales, evidence access, disputes, legal interpretation, collection delay and manual exceptions. R1 assigns zero active search budget. It may return only if a mechanism-level structural change removes those burdens rather than renaming the service.

### F11 — Demand-owning lead or referral assets

Owning demand can create durable cash flow, but building or acquiring verified demand usually requires capital, time and platform exposure. It conflicts with the current one-week pessimistic recovery preference and low irreversible-capital limit. It remains a watch mechanism, not an active frontier lane.

### By regional evidence lane

The existing geographic rule remains unchanged:

| Lane | Initial share |
|---|---:|
| A — China domestic buyers and domestic demand | 50% |
| B — Chinese capabilities serving global demand | 25% |
| C — Global projects remotely operable from China | 25% |

Domestic platform ecosystems are real but must not be treated as frictionless. WPS supports third-party enterprise apps that can be purchased and installed by multiple tenants, while its official listing process also requires service-provider certification, review, marketplace materials, pricing configuration, customer-service systems and operating plans. Feishu supports public store apps with review. Therefore the China lane should search both platform-native and standalone mechanisms, rather than assuming domestic marketplace distribution is automatically low-maintenance.

## 6. Mechanism concentration controls

No single family may receive more than 22% of the next search batch.

At least six orthogonal families must remain represented until the global frontier stop test is reached.

The following must be tracked by family:

- signal count;
- search attention;
- evidence spend;
- score-weighted share;
- projected revenue exposure;
- correlated failure-risk exposure.

A family cannot evade concentration control by changing industry labels.

## 7. Depth prohibition

R1 grants **zero candidate-specific depth licences**.

The following remain prohibited:

- named product selection;
- feature specification;
- price testing;
- page or prototype creation;
- competitor-by-competitor deep dives;
- outreach;
- payment or KYC activation;
- customer-data collection;
- product build.

## 8. Conditions for the next stage

The only legal next stage is:

`PAC-51-R29F-META-R5H-GLOBAL-MECHANISM-FRONTIER-R2-SIGNAL-SWEEP`

R2 may collect 30–50 mechanism-level signals per batch under the regional and family budgets above. A signal must describe a repeatable payment-and-delivery structure, not a named product proposal.

R2 must stop or reallocate attention when:

- structural failure evidence arrives;
- the family repeatedly produces only custom service or enterprise-sales structures;
- support, legal or integration tails dominate owner cash flow;
- additional signals no longer change the frontier ranking;
- one family begins to consume attention because it is easy to imagine rather than because evidence quality is improving.

No family advances to a named candidate until the cross-mechanism Global Top-tier Sufficiency Gate and Action Value Gate both pass.
